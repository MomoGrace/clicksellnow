How to Compress PDF Files Online for Free

PDF files are commonly used for documents, school work, forms, reports, invoices and applications. However, large PDF files can be difficult to upload, email or share online. If your PDF file is too large, compressing it can help reduce the file size and make it easier to use.

In this guide, you will learn why PDF files become large, when you should compress them, and how to use a free online PDF compression tool.

Why Are PDF Files So Large?

PDF files can become large for several reasons. They may contain high-resolution images, scanned pages, graphics, forms or many pages. A scanned document is often much larger than a text-based PDF because each page may be saved as an image.

Why Compress a PDF File?

Compressing a PDF file can make it easier to send, upload and store. Many websites, email services and application forms have file size limits. A smaller PDF file is also faster to upload and easier to share.

  • Send PDF files by email more easily.
  • Upload documents to online forms faster.
  • Save storage space on your device.
  • Share files with fewer size problems.

Tips Before Compressing PDF Files

Before compressing a PDF, check whether the document contains important images or details. Very strong compression may reduce image quality. For documents that need to remain clear, it is usually better to use moderate compression.

When Should You Compress a PDF?

You should compress a PDF when the file is too large for email, online uploads or storage. This is especially useful for scanned documents, forms, reports and image-heavy files.
